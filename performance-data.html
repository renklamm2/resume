How These Outcomes Get Built

1. Diagnostic before instruction

Every intervention block opens with a fresh look at Amplify mCLASS sub-skill data, recent classroom work, and any DIBELS / VALLS results. Instruction is targeted at the specific sub-skill blocking growth — not a generic grade-level review.

2. Weekly progress monitoring, weekly adjustments

Students are progress-monitored every week in Amplify. If two consecutive data points are flat, the lesson plan changes — new modality, smaller skill chunk, different scaffold — before a third week is wasted.

3. Tight loop with the classroom teacher

Intervention only sticks when it’s reinforced in the core classroom. I bring weekly progress summaries to the classroom teacher and the Reading Interventionist so push-in supports, small-group rotations, and homework all pull in the same direction.

4. Confidence built on visible wins

Students see their own growth charts. Naming the win out loud — “three weeks ago you couldn’t do this, today you did” — is the single most reliable lever I’ve found for shifting attitude alongside skill.
